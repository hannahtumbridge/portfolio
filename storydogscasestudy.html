Project Type

A practical team project with 3 other members of the UX/UI Bootcamp I attended - focusing on a User Interface Design “uplift” for a local non-for-profit organization. My team and I selected Story Dogs, an Australian NFP focused on improving the literacy skills of all children.

My Role

During a collaborative project between the 4 participants, I focused on the UX elements of the project's development process and UI design related components to our branding and styling.



Our Challenge



The Problem can be summarised into the simple statement: “The significance of the work done by the team at Story Dogs is weakened by the outdated design of the current website.”

From this we could extract several key elements that we felt like we could achieve within our time available for this project. These were:

  • Improve site navigation system
  • Implement a sitemap that works for all Users
  • Fix inconsistent User Flows
  • Build an internal payment/sign up portal
  • Improve colour accessibility
  • Build a more professional UI system
  • Ensure the uplift adhears to Responsive Web Design

    Our group approached our uplift project as a multi-step process. The main focus was to achieve a worthy update to the site within our current timeframe of 3 weeks. We broke down our process into 5 stages - Research, Define, Ideate, Prototype and Test, however our flow through these stages wasn’t necessarily linear.

    Whilst we did start with researching the User’s that we involved in the site and performing a heuristic evaluation - we also focused on pulling together a style guide from their current social media branding or some beginning wireframes that were based off our competitor analysis.

    Working in a team meant that we could sub-divide the work across multiple fronts and move quickly through it to ensure that we met our deadline and ensure that we didn't experience too much scope creep.

Due to the timeframe we knew we wouldn't be able to achieve all of the goals they had but focused on these major ones:

    We would like the redesign to show how our organization has impacted over 330 schools across Australia.

    We are most successful in rural & regional areas and populations that have a higher number of retirees.

    Our biggest strength is that our program is low cost for both volunteers and schools.
